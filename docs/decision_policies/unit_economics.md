# Unit Economics

Unit economics define whether a model action is worth taking.

## Components

| Component | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Spread capture | Revenue from accepted flow. |
| Adverse-selection cost | Future price movement against passive execution. |
| Hedge cost | Cost of reducing inventory or market exposure. |
| Missed revenue | Lost value from rejection or worse quote acceptance. |
| Operational cost | Per-action cost, if modeled. |
| Risk penalty | Cost assigned to inventory, stress, or drawdown. |

## Reporting Rule

Every economic policy report must state units, costs, notional assumptions, and whether the outcome is simulated, observed proxy, or realized PnL.
